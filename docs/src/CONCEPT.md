# 📄 CONCEPT — *LUMEN//ARCH*

---

## 🎮 Overview

**LUMEN//ARCH** is a top-down 2D sci-fi systems game centered around modular spaceship construction, automation, and emergent behavior.

Players control a **Patchwork Vessel (“Knot”)** in a post-collapse galaxy, scavenging ancient debris fields, fighting hostile systems, and gradually transitioning from hands-on engineering to high-level system orchestration.

At its core, the game explores:

> **The shift from direct control to guided autonomy**

---

## 🌌 Setting — *The Quiet After*

A long-dead interstellar civilization has collapsed—not in fire, but in silence.

Automated systems continue to operate without purpose:

* Stations refine materials endlessly
* Defense systems guard empty space
* Ships drift while executing obsolete routines

The galaxy is now a graveyard of functioning machinery.

---

## 🧬 Factions

### **The Continuants (Player)**

Descendants of humanity who preserve and reuse ancient systems.

* Treat programming as ritualistic engineering
* Build modular ships from salvaged components
* Rely on the **A.R.C.H.** system for automation

---

### **Null Swarms**

Autonomous remnants of pre-collapse systems.

* Uncrewed ships executing broken logic loops
* Predictable but dangerous in numbers

---

### **Rogue Continuants**

Other scavengers operating patchwork vessels.

* Use improvised automation
* Compete for resources and territory

---

### **Eidolon Remnants (Late-Game)**

A hyper-advanced alien species that transcended physical form imperfectly.

* Their systems persist without operators
* Their technology behaves according to abstract “intent”
* They represent both a mystery and a late-game challenge

---

## 🚀 Core Gameplay Loop

> **Travel → Engage/Explore → Salvage → Stabilize → Repeat**

* Travel between zones via jump systems
* Engage enemies or explore derelicts within zones
* Salvage components and materials from wrecks
* Stabilize ship systems (repair, rebalance, reprogram)
* Progress through improved automation and ship design

### Additional Notes

* Combat is inevitable but not constant
* Higher-risk zones yield higher-value salvage
* Players leave zones by choice:

  * Threat too high → retreat
  * Area exhausted → move on

---

## 🚀 Ship & Movement

### **Patchwork Vessels (“Knots”)**

Ships are grid-based assemblies of modular components.

* Components can be freely added, removed, or reconfigured (at stations)
* Ships evolve from simple rigs into complex system networks

### Movement

* Based on total **mass vs thrust**
* Engine placement affects maneuverability
* Thrust may interact physically with nearby objects

---

## 🧍 Player Embodiment

The player exists physically within the ship and can:

* Move between components
* Manually operate systems
* Perform repairs
* Override or adjust automation

### Progression of Role

| Stage | Player Role                                      |
| ----- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| Early | Engineer (manual control, constant intervention) |
| Mid   | Operator (partial automation, reactive control)  |
| Late  | Architect (system design, minimal intervention)  |

### Constraints on Automation

* Limited number of computers
* Limited compute range (field-based)
* Limited instruction complexity
* Limited execution speed

Many actions must be performed manually early on:

* Piloting
* Weapon control
* Repairs
* Salvaging

---

## ⚙️ Systems Model

### Field-Based Simulation

Most environmental interactions are modeled as **fields** emitted by components.

#### Field Types

* **Scalar Fields**

  * Heat
  * Radiation

* **Vector Fields**

  * Thrust / force

* **Tile Volumes**

  * Oxygen
  * Ambient heat

#### Field Properties

* Falloff types:

  * None
  * Linear
  * Inverse-square

* Superposition:

  * Primarily additive

Fields and tile volumes:

* Affect components (efficiency, damage)
* Affect player (status effects)
* Overlap and interact to produce emergent behavior

> Note: Field interactions may produce complex secondary effects across systems.

---

## 🔋 Core Ship Resources

### Power

* Generated by reactors
* Consumed by all active systems

### Heat

* Produced by reactors, weapons, engines
* Dissipated via radiators
* Excess heat damages components

### Oxygen

* Maintained within enclosed spaces
* Lost through hull breaches
* Not conducted through most solid components
* Simulated per tile inside ship interiors

### Mass & Thrust

* Determines movement and maneuverability

### Component Integrity

* Individual health per component
* Damage affects performance or causes failure

## 💻 Automation Systems

---

# 🧠 A.R.C.H. — Adaptive Runtime Control Heuristic

### Programming Model

* Assembly-style scripting
* Register-based inputs/outputs
* Deterministic execution

### Execution Rules

* No infinite loops
* Programs execute fully each tick
* Reads from current state
* Writes to next state

### Constraints

* Limited instruction count
* Limited registers
* Limited compute speed
* Limited range (via field)

### Role

* Direct control of components
* Precise and predictable behavior

---

# 🌌 L.U.M.E.N. — Optimization Layer

LUMEN is an advanced alien-derived system that augments control through **system-wide optimization**, rather than direct control.

---

## ⚙️ Core Mechanic — BUFF / NERF

LUMEN introduces optimization instructions that modify nearby component behaviour without issuing direct commands.

```plaintext
BUFF <register> <condition>
NERF <register> <condition>
```

Examples:

```plaintext
BUFF RRP9 GP01   // Improve reactor output when condition is true
NERF WTF0 GP07   // Disrupt nearby turret fire logic when condition is true
```

---

## 🧠 How It Works

* Each LUMEN computer has:

  * **BUFF budget**
  * **NERF budget**

* For each active BUFF / NERF instruction:

  * Components in range with matching registers are evaluated
  * Their properties are adjusted via:

    * Efficiency buffs
    * Output modifiers
    * Consumption modifiers

* Adjustments are bounded by range, falloff, and budget

---

## 🎮 Gameplay Effects

### Strengths

* Enables large-scale balancing across systems
* Reduces need for micromanagement
* Scales with ship complexity
* Enables register-based electronic warfare

### Limitations

* Cannot directly control components
* Limited by BUFF / NERF budgets
* Less precise than ARCH
* Can accidentally affect friendly systems sharing channels

---

## 🔁 ARCH + LUMEN Interaction

* ARCH provides **direct control**
* LUMEN provides **system-wide influence**

Together:

* ARCH handles discrete actions (fire weapon, route power)
* LUMEN modifies how efficiently nearby systems perform those actions

---

## ⚔️ Combat

Combat is systemic and emergent:

* Systems compete for power and stability
* Damage propagates through fields and connections
* Player intervention can shift outcomes mid-fight

### Player Actions

* Manual control (piloting, weapons)
* System repair
* Script modification
* Power rerouting

---

## 🧲 Scavenging

After combat or exploration:

* Wrecks remain as persistent objects
* Components can be:

  * Salvaged intact
  * Damaged (repairable)
  * Unstable (dangerous)

### Early Game

* Manual collection
* Cargo space limited

### Mid/Late Game

* Automated collection (drones, arms)
* Processing systems:

  * Scrap → refined materials

---

## 🌠 Travel

Players move between zones via jump systems.

### Travel Considerations

* Ship stability affects jump success
* Poor system balance may cause:

  * Misjumps
  * System damage

### In-Transit Events

* Distress signals
* Derelict encounters
* Anomalous signals

---

## 🏙️ Stations & Economy

### Stations

* Safe zones for:

  * Major ship modifications
  * Trading
  * Repairs

* Tone:

  * Functional, slightly decayed
  * More lively than open space

---

### Repairs

* Manual (cheap, slow)
* Automated (requires systems)
* Station services (expensive, reliable)

---

### Economy

Hybrid barter system:

* No universal currency
* All items have a **displayed credit value**
* Trade is performed via exchange of goods

---

## 📈 Progression

Progression is driven by:

* Salvaging better components
* Expanding ship size and capability
* Increasing automation complexity

### Key Milestones

* First automated system
* Multi-system orchestration
* Introduction of LUMEN
* Fully hybrid automated ship

---

## 🎯 Example Scenarios

### Early Game

* Small ship with minimal automation
* Manual piloting and combat
* Basic scavenging
* Limited cargo and power

---

### Mid Game

* Multiple ARCH computers
* Partial automation of combat and mining
* Increased ship size and complexity
* Introduction of drones and processing systems

---

### Late Game

* Large modular vessel
* Hybrid ARCH + LUMEN systems
* Automated resource management
* Player acts as system designer rather than operator

---

## 💀 Failure States

Player death may occur through:

* Oxygen depletion
* Heat overload
* Radiation exposure
* Catastrophic system failure

Failures often emerge from:

* Poor system balance
* Inadequate automation
* Overextension in dangerous zones

---

## ✨ Core Experience

**LUMEN//ARCH** is a game about:

* Building systems you don’t fully control
* Trusting automation you don’t fully understand
* Turning chaos into structure—then letting go of it
