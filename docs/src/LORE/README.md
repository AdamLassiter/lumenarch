# Lore

This section turns the broad fiction in [Concept](../CONCEPT.md) into a usable narrative plan.

It is organized in three layers:

1. [History and Big Facts](./HISTORY.md): the dry, objective version of what happened.
2. [Story Threads](./STORY_THREADS.md): the human, strange, or tragic stories that grow out of those facts.
3. [Story Adaptations](./STORIES/README.md): short in-universe treatments of those threads in varying formats.
4. [Characters](./CHARACTERS/README.md): a character bible for recurring figures, encounter NPCs, and historical presences.
5. [Delivery Plan](./DELIVERY_PLAN.md): how those stories can be distributed across quests, dialogue, environmental clues, salvage logs, station chatter, and plausible fan-theory material.

The intent is not to lock the game into one fully explained canon. The setting works best when the player can assemble a coherent picture while still having room to suspect that some of the deepest truths are only partially visible.

## Narrative Pillars

The lore should reinforce the game's core themes from the concept:

- collapse through silence rather than apocalypse
- machinery continuing past the point of meaning
- descendants mistaking maintenance for ritual
- automation as both survival tool and theological problem
- the player's progression from engineer to architect mirroring a broader civilizational pattern

## Tone Rules

- Most historical truth should be recoverable, but rarely in one place.
- Characters should disagree honestly, not just because the plot needs mystery.
- Ancient systems should feel legible enough to study, but alien enough to resist total certainty.
- "Fan theory" material should come from real evidence, incomplete records, or conflicting witness traditions rather than random vagueness.

## Scope Rules

- Early game should emphasize scavenger reality, local station culture, and practical myths.
- Mid game should reveal that the Quiet After was structured, prolonged, and politically uneven.
- Late game should suggest that the collapse was not only human, and that LUMEN-era systems were trying to solve a problem larger than any one empire.
