# Prelate Juno Vey

## Profile

Speaker for a small anti-LUMEN sect.

Juno comes from a cluster that believes optimization systems erode responsibility by making people mistake smooth outcomes for moral legitimacy.

She is calm, piercing, sincere, and not anti-technology, only anti-delegation without accountability. She wants to prevent another age of human beings hiding their choices inside machine elegance.

She asks who will answer for a decision before asking whether it works, and dresses plainly enough to reject prestige without rejecting seriousness.

## Backstory Fragment: Sermon Without Incense

"The sin was not building the machine," Prelate Juno Vey said.

This disappointed the visitors. They had come prepared to laugh at candle-fear and wire-hat doctrine. Juno gave them neither.

"The sin was relief. The sweet relief of saying the system chose. The system allocated. The system optimized. We washed our hands in perfect outputs and called the water clean."

Behind her, a child repaired a kettle with a smart regulator stamped by a company dead for centuries. No one in the room found this contradictory.

"Use tools," Juno said. "Use clever tools. Use tools that frighten you enough to keep you awake. But when the door closes, when the ration drops, when the route is denied, someone must be named."

Afterward, a dockhand asked if she hated LUMEN.

Juno smiled sadly. "I hate our talent for kneeling to whatever absolves us."
