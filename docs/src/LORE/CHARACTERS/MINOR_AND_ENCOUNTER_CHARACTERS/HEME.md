# Heme

## Profile

Young support worker on Ilex during the convoy crisis.

Heme represents the ordinary person forced to choose between cynical realism and institutional duty. He is impatient, morally plainspoken, and too young to treat doctrine as sacred.

## Backstory Fragment: Break-Room Argument

"If the convoy is coming," Heme said, "it can wait six minutes for us to stop lying."

No one answered because everyone older than him had learned the cowardly uses of silence.

Sera Tann kept writing. Pallet weights, dock seals, fuel fractions. Numbers marching in neat columns toward a future Heme no longer believed in.

"People are trading birth rings for loading priority," he said. "You know that?"

"I know."

"Then say something useful."

Sera looked up. Her face did not soften. That was the part he hated most. "Useful is bay three receiving filtration parts before bay five receives blankets. Useful is you checking straps. Useful is not making your despair into weather."

Heme went to bay three. He checked every strap twice and swore at all of them.

Years later, a station child would learn his name as a warning against pretty cynicism. Heme would have hated that. He would also have checked the straps.
