# Ilen

## Profile

Witness and letter-writer from the Last Mutual Aid War.

Ilen is valuable not because he is powerful, but because he refuses clean myths. He is thoughtful, politically exhausted, and brave enough to describe small causes behind large wars.

## Backstory Fragment: Letter to Mira

Mira,

The first shot was not about water.

That is what they will say because water sounds noble and inevitable. The first shot was about a delayed audit, a missing pump seal, two men embarrassed in public, and a council that had spent three years pretending resentment was logistics.

Water came later, as it always does. So did flags. So did songs.

I watched Harlan Voss die with a wrench in his hand and no idea which slogan would claim him. His brother now says Harlan died for free passage. His mother says he died because nobody checked the east manifold when she told them to. I believe his mother.

If we survive this, do not let them polish it. A polished war becomes a tool that fits too comfortably in the next hand.

I miss arguing with you about smaller failures.

Ilen
