# Dockmother Es Kerrow

## Profile

Mechanic-priest figure on a middling refit station.

Es runs a service ring where customers come for repairs, informal arbitration, and the sort of advice people only accept when it arrives covered in grease.

She is warm until crossed, theatrical when blessing machinery, and ruthlessly concrete when quoting prices. She wants to keep her dock indispensable enough that bigger stations leave it alone.

She speaks to ships before speaking to crews, flicks broken fasteners into a jar while thinking, and uses affectionate insults as customer triage.

## Backstory Fragment: Dockside Rumor

Dockmother Es blessed my pump with a hammer.

She kissed the casing first, called it a tired little saint, then hit it hard enough to make my pilot swear in three dialects. The pump started. Es named a price. I objected. She named a higher price for insulting a miracle.

People say she was born under a dry lock during a pressure alarm. People say her first word was "invoice." People say a Latchwell inspector once tried to condemn her service ring and left with a repaired knee brace, a fear of fastener jars, and a receipt marked DONATION.

Truth is simpler. Es grew up watching larger stations buy small docks by letting them fail one contract at a time. So she made herself useful, loud, beloved, expensive, and slightly holy.

No one annexes a shrine while their ship is still on its blocks.
