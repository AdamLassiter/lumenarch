# Rell

## Profile

Dock associate who assisted Braddan during the knife-crate confrontation.

Rell shows how station discipline is often collective rather than heroic. They are quiet, exact, and good at making implied threats look procedural.

## Backstory Fragment: Witness Statement

Rell never raised their voice.

That is what people remember incorrectly as calm. Calm is a feeling. Rell was a procedure with boots.

When the knife-crate crew tried to push past Braddan's count, Rell stepped between the second loader and the dock release. They did not draw a weapon. They placed a stamped denial chit on the man's chest and said, "Your cargo is now educational material."

The loader laughed.

Rell smiled, which every dockhand knew was worse than a siren, and recited the seizure order exactly. Clause, exception, lockout code. By the time the loader realized the threat had already happened, three apprentices had chained the crate and Toma was bleeding cheerfully under the latch.

Later stories give Braddan all the thunder. Rell became a shadow in the mural, one hand on a lever.

That is fair. Shadows show where the light was blocked.
