# Hadrik Sol

## Profile

Retired route gunner and bar fixture.

Hadrik survived enough convoy work to become both valuable and exhausting. He knows old lane behavior, remembers obsolete ship classes, and drinks like a man negotiating with memory.

He is gruff, observant, theatrical when drunk, and exact when sober. He wants his experience to matter one last time.

He identifies wreck types from silhouettes, pauses before naming the dead, and hates people who speak casually about retreat.

## Backstory Fragment: Bar Recording, Permission Unclear

"That was a Rill-class tender," Hadrik said, pointing through the viewport at a wreck no one else could classify.

Fen asked how he knew.

"Ugly shoulders."

He was drunk enough to rhyme badly and sober enough to remember weapon arcs. That was Hadrik's permanent weather. He named the tender, the year, the convoy song, then stopped before the crew list and stared into his cup like the names were waiting at the bottom.

"People think retreat is a direction," he said. "Retreat is a debt. You pay it now or someone pays it later with interest."

Later that night he stood in the corridor and corrected a patrol cadet's firing stance with such tenderness that everyone pretended not to see.

The next morning he denied the tenderness and identified three more wrecks before breakfast.
