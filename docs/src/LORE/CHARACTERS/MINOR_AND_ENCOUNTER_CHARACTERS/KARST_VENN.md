# Karst Venn

## Profile

Bandit captain who insists he is a tollkeeper.

Karst comes from a station chain that collapsed after being cut out of route maintenance agreements. He now runs a violent salvage toll operation in a corridor he claims his people once kept safe for everyone.

He is articulate, bitter, charismatic in short bursts, and prone to building legal language around extortion. He wants recognition that infrastructure labor creates ownership, even if he has to express that thesis with armed boarding actions.

He cites obsolete docking law before threatening violence, dresses in repaired civic colors to make a point, and gets angrier when called a pirate than when shot at.

## Backstory Fragment: Boarding Declaration

"This is not piracy," Karst Venn said over the open channel. "Piracy is theft without maintenance history."

The merchant captain made the mistake of laughing.

Karst's ship wore patched civic colors from three dead stations, red over green over a blue so faded it looked bruised. His crew moved like dockhands who had learned guns after ledgers stopped mattering.

"Your route crosses Venn-chain service space," he continued. "Our grandparents kept these beacons alive while your insurers called us provisional. Your cargo will contribute to arrears."

"You mean tribute."

"I mean arrears."

Later, when the shooting ended badly for everyone, Karst sat beside a cracked beacon housing and cleaned blood from an old municipal badge. He did not look triumphant. He looked like a man still waiting for a court that had vented itself generations ago.
