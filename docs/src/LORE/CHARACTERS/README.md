# Characters

This section is a working character bible for the setting.

It includes:

- people already named in the story adaptations
- figures implied by the broader history
- practical game-facing characters such as mechanics, fixers, scavengers, and bandits
- a few prominent historical or ideological figures who may never appear directly, but whose presence can shape the world

The goal is not to define every line these people will ever speak. It is to establish a strong enough core that they can recur across dialogue, contracts, rumors, environmental storytelling, and collectible records without flattening into exposition devices.

## Design Goals

- Every major character should want something concrete, not just symbolize a theme.
- Even minor encounter NPCs should suggest a life larger than their current scene.
- Continuants should feel culturally specific: practical, inherited, slightly ritualized, and suspicious of systems that pretend to be effortless.
- Ideological conflict should be personal before it becomes abstract.
