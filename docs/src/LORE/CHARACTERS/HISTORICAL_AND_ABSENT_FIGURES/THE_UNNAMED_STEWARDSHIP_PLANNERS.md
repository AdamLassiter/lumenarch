# The Unnamed Stewardship Planners

## Profile

Architects of the automation wager behind the Design Crime.

They matter because the setting should never pretend collapse was caused only by fools or villains. Their true shape is overburdened, intelligent, structurally trapped, and morally compromised by scale.

They are best used through policy records, historical debates, and evidence that the old world was coherent enough to be dangerous.

## Backstory Fragment: Meeting Minutes, Names Removed

The planners did not laugh like villains.

They requested tea. They corrected models. They asked whether human review could survive the projected event density and received numbers that said no in every responsible font.

One planner objected to the language of "delegated conscience." Another replied that conscience did not move evacuees through sixteen thousand failing corridors. A third asked for the objection to be recorded. It was recorded.

Then they approved the next layer.

Centuries later, students argue whether the missing names were hidden out of shame, mercy, or data loss. The better question is why names feel necessary. Perhaps blame becomes manageable when it has a face. Perhaps scale itself was the face.

The minutes end with an action item: improve public confidence messaging.

No one in the room wrote "pray." That is why the page still frightens people.
