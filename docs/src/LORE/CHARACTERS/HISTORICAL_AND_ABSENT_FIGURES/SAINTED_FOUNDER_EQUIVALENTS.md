# Sainted Founder Equivalents

## Profile

A class of people rather than one person: mechanics, quartermasters, route captains, and dock chiefs later elevated into civic ancestors.

They show how different stations mythologize different virtues: fairness, caution, aggression, hospitality, and stubborn continuity.

They are best used through station-specific festivals, plaques, sayings, and local lore flavor that implies deep time.

## Backstory Fragment: Plaques from Three Stations

### Redhook Founding Plaque

Orren Pell welded the first public lock while raiders cut the outer skin. He is shown holding a torch in one hand and a ration chit in the other, which is historically unlikely and politically efficient.

Children touch the torch before exams.

### Mercy-of-Glass Kitchen Shrine

Founder Lysa fed strangers before cousins and became a saint of hospitality after the cousins survived anyway. Her feast day requires one guest no one trusts.

The soup is deliberately thin. Abundance would ruin the lesson.

### Latchwell Caution Stone

No face. No name. Only a carved hand above a manual valve.

The inscription reads: somebody said no.

Locals leave broken switches there, not flowers.

### Common Saying

Every station gets the saint it can afford.
