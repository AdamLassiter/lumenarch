# The Blue-Veil Quartermaster

## Profile

Unknown official whose evac credentials survive in machine-recognizable fragments.

The Blue-Veil Quartermaster is a recurring ghost of bureaucracy: a person never met, but whose authority still opens doors. They are strongest in refinery plots, dead identity chains, and repeated metadata signatures.

## Backstory Fragment: Authentication Echoes

The machines remember the veil.

Not the face. Not the voice except in damaged administrative cadence. But the credential chain persists: blue-veil quartermaster, provisional evac authority, refineries three through nine, emergency override with compassion exemption pending.

Pending.

Aven Rusk says pending is the most powerful word in dead bureaucracy. It means the door has not decided whether time is over.

Some stations claim the Quartermaster was a woman who smuggled unregistered families through refinery intake. Others say a committee used a single ceremonial identity because the machines trusted persons more than councils. A sect on Greyhook insists the veil was not cloth but coolant vapor from a ruptured line, sanctified by useful coincidence.

The surviving logs offer only fragments:

authorize shelter

delay audit

accept liability

Whoever they were, they left the old world owing them an answer.
