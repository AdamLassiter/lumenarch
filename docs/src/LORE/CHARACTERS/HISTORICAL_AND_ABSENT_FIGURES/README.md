## Braddan Vale

### Role

Historical quartermaster later mythologized as the Salvager Saint.

### Importance

A perfect example of how competence becomes sanctity in Continuant memory.

### True Shape

- hard, fair, unsentimental
- mathematically moral
- skilled at turning order into survival

### Best Use

- paired records
- contradictory memory
- ritual observance grounded in practical crisis

## The Unnamed Stewardship Planners

### Role

Architects of the automation wager behind the Design Crime.

### Importance

They matter because the setting should never pretend collapse was caused only by fools or villains.

### True Shape

- overburdened
- intelligent
- structurally trapped
- morally compromised by scale

### Best Use

- policy records
- historical debates
- evidence that the old world was coherent enough to be dangerous

## The Blue-Veil Quartermaster

### Role

Unknown official whose evac credentials survive in machine-recognizable fragments.

### Importance

Useful recurring ghost of bureaucracy: a person never met, but whose authority still opens doors.

### Best Use

- refinery plots
- dead identity chains
- repeated metadata signatures

## The Preserved Subject

### Role

The person or pattern at the center of the Archivist Without a Body.

### Importance

More powerful if never stabilized into a single answer.

### Best Use

- a center of interpretation, not biography
- evidence of love, grief, or continuity without total explanation

## Sainted Founder Equivalents

### Role

A class of people rather than one person: mechanics, quartermasters, route captains, and dock chiefs later elevated into civic ancestors.

### Importance

Useful for showing how different stations mythologize different virtues:

- fairness
- caution
- aggression
- hospitality
- stubborn continuity

### Best Use

- station-specific festivals, plaques, and sayings
- local lore flavor that implies deep time
