## Sera Tann

### Role

Former dispatch supervisor of Ilex corridor support operations; in the present, remembered as a patron figure of convoy duty and disciplined maintenance.

### Background

Sera served during the late Quiet Break, when support stations were still trying to uphold evacuation doctrine despite broken command chains and missing fleet confirmation. She helped keep the corridor's provisioning systems alive long past the point where confidence was justified.

### Personality

- disciplined
- unsentimental
- dignified under impossible circumstances
- allergic to melodrama

### Motivation

Her central drive was to avoid becoming "the point of failure," even after she privately suspected the failure had already happened elsewhere.

### Mannerisms

- signs things carefully even when no one is watching
- answers emotional questions with doctrine first
- speaks as if every phrase should be able to survive being repeated later

### Narrative Use

- appears through logs, memorial language, or station traditions
- can anchor quests about dead convoy routes and corridor doctrine
- useful as a symbol of tragic competence rather than sainthood

### Secret / Complication

She may have known earlier than most that the convoy was never coming, but chose continuity anyway.

## Nera Vale

### Role

Quartermaster and safety elder of Spindle Mercy.

### Background

Raised in a station culture where maintenance law, oral tradition, and grief discipline were inseparable. Nera inherited procedures from people who survived real decompression disasters and power failures, and she treats every "rite" as a compressed survival technology.

### Personality

- dryly funny
- severe in training contexts
- deeply protective
- not interested in being admired

### Motivation

To keep useful truth alive in forms that people will actually remember under stress.

### Mannerisms

- teaches by making people repeat phrases aloud
- taps plaques, seals, and valves while thinking
- uses embarrassment as a training tool, but never cruelty

### Narrative Use

- excellent early-game mechanic / station mentor
- voice for Continuant ritualized practicality
- can give the player specific habits that later pay off in story or mechanics

### Secret / Complication

She worries that her generation is already turning survival law into empty performance and that she may be preserving the words better than the habits.

## Pio Marr

### Role

Young deckhand, later a bridge figure between inherited ritual and living practice.

### Background

Started as a skeptical apprentice amused by elder doctrine. Gradually becomes the sort of person who discovers, reluctantly, that the old warnings were written in blood.

### Personality

- curious
- initially glib
- eager to look unafraid
- learns seriousness the slow way

### Motivation

To become genuinely competent without turning into the kind of elder he once mocked.

### Mannerisms

- writes private marginal notes after public lessons
- jokes first, apologizes later
- repeats instructions under his breath when nervous

### Narrative Use

- good recurring "junior perspective" character
- useful for showing how station culture reproduces itself
- can grow across the game or between documents

## Aven Rusk

### Role

Credential broker, salvage-rights fixer, and scholar of dead bureaucracies.

### Background

Aven emerged from dockside economies built on spoofed identity chains, obsolete route law, and machine-recognized authority fragments. They know how to make old systems acknowledge living need without simply smashing them.

### Personality

- charming when useful
- vain about precision
- morally flexible in method, morally stubborn in self-justification
- half con artist, half archivist

### Motivation

To prove that inheritance belongs to the living clever enough to extract it, not to councils pretending they understand legitimacy better than old machines do.

### Mannerisms

- corrects terminology even in tense situations
- treats dead credentials like sacred calligraphy
- dresses practical clothes around one conspicuously elegant old accessory

### Narrative Use

- midgame contact for refinery, ownership, and salvage-law plots
- can open morally gray quest paths
- useful bridge between practical scavenging and bigger historical truths

### Secret / Complication

Aven genuinely believes they are redistributing dead wealth toward survival, but they also enjoy being one of the few people who can command old systems with the right words.

## Seli Dorn

### Role

Councilor and public philosopher of Latchwell, a sector that deliberately limits system scale.

### Background

Descended from communities that survived a local automation failure by aggressively redesigning for bounded trust. Seli is educated, politically sharp, and tired of being treated as a picturesque conservative by richer stations.

### Personality

- calm
- incisive
- patient until condescended to
- capable of making restraint sound radical

### Motivation

To defend small-scale survivability as an intelligent political choice, not a symptom of poverty or fear.

### Mannerisms

- answers questions with sharper questions
- insists on defining terms before arguing policy
- rarely raises her voice, which makes it land when she does

### Narrative Use

- ideological counterweight to player progress
- recurring station contact whose critiques actually matter
- good source of quests about trust, limits, and local autonomy

### Secret / Complication

She knows Latchwell's caution is materially costly and privately fears their model may only work because other regions still bear the risks of ambition.

## Yara Sen

### Role

Patrol captain, witness to the Null Mercy Incident.

### Background

Seasoned route captain with a reputation for clean sensor logs and conservative tactical judgment. Her credibility makes her strange report about Null escort behavior harder to dismiss completely.

### Personality

- concise
- pragmatic
- suspicious of romantic interpretations
- quietly haunted by edge cases

### Motivation

To understand what she saw without allowing hope to soften operational judgment.

### Mannerisms

- trims stories down until others force detail out of her
- distrusts people who sound certain about machines
- checks line-of-retreat options in every room

### Narrative Use

- excellent contact for patrol, convoy, or route-security quests
- can introduce machine ambiguity without sentimentalizing it
- works well as a recurring skeptic whose skepticism is earned

### Secret / Complication

Part of her wants the HX-11 event to mean something humane survived in the machines, and she distrusts that part of herself.

## Tal Iri

### Role

Research operator and translator of LUMEN-adjacent ruins.

### Background

Tal came up through salvage interpretation rather than pure academy culture, which makes them unusually willing to admit confusion instead of hiding behind jargon. They are one of the few people comfortable saying that some truths may be conceptually adjacent to human understanding rather than simply withheld from it.

### Personality

- attentive
- intellectually honest
- reverent without being credulous
- emotionally affected by evidence

### Motivation

To make contact with non-human systems without reducing them to either treasure or ghost stories.

### Mannerisms

- pauses before giving names to things
- keeps multiple translations alive at once
- sounds most certain when describing limits, not conclusions

### Narrative Use

- late-midgame LUMEN guide or correspondent
- useful voice for turning mysteries into structured questions
- can humanize deep-lore investigation without flattening the alien

### Secret / Complication

Tal worries that every successful translation into human categories may also be a kind of vandalism.

## Mira

### Role

Off-screen but emotionally important figure tied to the Last Mutual Aid War correspondence.

### Background

Likely a negotiator, colleague, confidante, or estranged partner of Ilen during the final days of the mutual aid pact. Mira matters because the letter to her implies a relationship deep enough to carry the unsimplified truth.

### Personality

Reserved in records, but can be inferred as someone trusted with nuance.

### Motivation

Depends on how she is later developed:
she may want reconciliation, institutional truth, or merely a life not consumed by founding grievances.

### Narrative Use

- can remain half-seen
- can appear through replies, archived testimony, or descendants who preserve her view
- useful for turning one-sided historical documents into real dialogue

## Tovan

### Role

Expedition specialist whose curiosity tips into surrender before alien coherence.

### Background

A salvage professional with enough technical confidence to be genuinely dangerous in LUMEN-adjacent contexts. Tovan represents the kind of person who mistakes elegant response for benign intent.

### Personality

- exuberant
- technically gifted
- impatient with caution
- attracted to the feeling of being chosen by a system

### Motivation

To be first through the door that makes everyone else hesitate.

### Mannerisms

- grins at machines when they respond
- narrates risk like a challenge instead of a warning
- moves closer when others stop

### Narrative Use

- cautionary figure in expedition plots
- could appear only in reports, or return changed if a stranger tone is wanted

### Secret / Complication

Tovan may not be dead. Being missing is narratively stronger.

## Jessa

### Role

Field intuition specialist; the first to name a place as wrong before data catches up.

### Background

Comes from hazardous salvage culture, where reading atmosphere, crew tension, and environmental intention matters as much as instrumentation. Jessa is the counterweight to people who trust clean readings too easily.

### Personality

- sharp
- physically brave
- not embarrassed by intuition
- intolerant of macho stupidity

### Motivation

To survive long enough that her instincts stop being treated as superstition by men who want miracles.

### Mannerisms

- notices absence before presence
- touches walls, air currents, and seams like they can answer back
- swears briefly and precisely

### Narrative Use

- expedition companion
- salvage-zone voice line model
- strong minor character who can steal scenes with a sentence
