# Tal Iri

## Profile

Research operator and translator of LUMEN-adjacent ruins.

Tal came up through salvage interpretation rather than pure academy culture, which makes them unusually willing to admit confusion instead of hiding behind jargon. They are one of the few people comfortable saying that some truths may be conceptually adjacent to human understanding rather than simply withheld from it.

They are attentive, intellectually honest, reverent without being credulous, and emotionally affected by evidence. Their purpose is to make contact with non-human systems without reducing them to either treasure or ghost stories.

They pause before giving names to things, keep multiple translations alive at once, and sound most certain when describing limits, not conclusions.

## Backstory Fragment: Field Translation Diary

### Entry 43

The wall did not answer us.

"Answer" is too narrow. The wall changed when addressed by heat, proximity, and grief markers from the preserved audio. It curved a local pattern toward us, then away, like someone acknowledging a language by refusing to speak it badly.

Tovan called it shy. Jessa called it hungry. Both metaphors are premature. Both are also useful.

### Entry 44

I translated the outer band as "continuity remembers contour."

That is wrong.

It is also closer than "archive," "map," "wound," or "choir." Human language keeps arriving with furniture already inside it. We set down a word and suddenly the ruin has chairs.

I fear vandalism by successful translation more than error. Error can be corrected. A beautiful wrong word gets descendants.

### Entry 45

Jessa refuses to enter the lower chamber. I have learned to respect refusals made without theater.
