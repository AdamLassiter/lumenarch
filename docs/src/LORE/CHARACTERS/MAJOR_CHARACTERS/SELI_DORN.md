# Seli Dorn

## Profile

Councilor and public philosopher of Latchwell, a sector that deliberately limits system scale.

Seli descends from communities that survived a local automation failure by aggressively redesigning for bounded trust. She is educated, politically sharp, and tired of being treated as a picturesque conservative by richer stations.

She is calm, incisive, patient until condescended to, and capable of making restraint sound radical. Her purpose is to defend small-scale survivability as an intelligent political choice, not a symptom of poverty or fear.

She answers questions with sharper questions, insists on defining terms before arguing policy, and rarely raises her voice, which makes it land when she does.

## Backstory Fragment: Council Notes and a Private Recording

### Latchwell Public Forum

"Councilor Dorn," the visiting architect said, "surely your people understand that integrated optimization reduces waste."

Seli folded her hands. "Define waste."

"Lost throughput. Idle reserves. Duplicate labor."

"Then you have named three things that saved my grandmother."

The chamber went quiet in the satisfied way of rooms that know the knife has entered cleanly.

### Private Recording, Unsigned

I make restraint sound easier than it is.

Our children leave for brighter rings and write home about auto-docks that hum like choirs. Our doctors borrow machines we would never build. Our farms survive partly because ambitious neighbors take risks we lecture them about afterward.

This does not make us wrong. It does mean our virtue has plumbing outside the wall.

Tomorrow I will argue against sector integration again. I will be correct. I will also count the medicines we cannot synthesize alone.
