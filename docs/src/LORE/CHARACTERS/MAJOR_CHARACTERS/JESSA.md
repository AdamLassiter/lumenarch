# Jessa

## Profile

Field intuition specialist; the first to name a place as wrong before data catches up.

Jessa comes from hazardous salvage culture, where reading atmosphere, crew tension, and environmental intention matters as much as instrumentation. She is the counterweight to people who trust clean readings too easily.

She is sharp, physically brave, not embarrassed by intuition, and intolerant of macho stupidity. She wants to survive long enough that her instincts stop being treated as superstition by men who want miracles.

She notices absence before presence, touches walls, air currents, and seams like they can answer back, and swears briefly and precisely.

## Backstory Fragment: Self-Monologue in a Sealed Lock

The instruments say clean.

The instruments are adorable.

Clean air can still be wrong air. Floors can invite weight. Doors can wait like they have been told a joke and want to see if you get it. Nobody who has pulled a friend out of a smiling wreck thinks silence is neutral.

Tovan says I give rooms motives. Tal says "motive" is a human category and then backs up when I tell them to back up, because Tal is clever in the useful way.

I learned this on a nursery ship with green walls. My uncle walked through first because the scanner blessed it. I was twelve. I remember the exact sound of a safe corridor deciding to become two corridors.

So when I say the chamber is wrong, I do not mean haunted. I mean I can feel the old math leaning.

If the others follow Tovan, I will follow them. Brave is not the opposite of afraid. Brave is being afraid with a rope clipped on.
