# Sera Tann

## Profile

Former dispatch supervisor of Ilex corridor support operations. In the present, remembered as a patron figure of convoy duty and disciplined maintenance.

Sera served during the late Quiet Break, when support stations were still trying to uphold evacuation doctrine despite broken command chains and missing fleet confirmation. She helped keep the corridor's provisioning systems alive long past the point where confidence was justified.

She is disciplined, unsentimental, dignified under impossible circumstances, and allergic to melodrama. Her central drive was to avoid becoming "the point of failure," even after she privately suspected the failure had already happened elsewhere.

She signs things carefully even when no one is watching, answers emotional questions with doctrine first, and speaks as if every phrase should be able to survive being repeated later.

## Backstory Fragment: Dispatch Log Redactions

### Ilex Support Dispatch, Day 811

The corridor bell failed again. Heme wanted to strike it with a wrench. I told him bells are not repaired by anger, though I have seen worse procedures approved by fleet command.

Three freighters requested priority oxygen allotment. All three carried children. The doctrine is clear: evacuation craft by launch timestamp, not by pleading. I signed the schedule, watched the quartermaster stamp it, then changed nothing.

I do not know if that makes me disciplined or merely obedient to a dead room.

### Personal Addendum, Not Transmitted

There is no fleet confirmation. There has been no fleet confirmation for fourteen cycles. I have begun writing the convoy timings in black ink instead of blue because blue still looks like a promise.

The younger hands ask whether the fleet is coming. The older hands ask whether we should keep loading pallets. The second question is the only one with an answer.

Load the pallets.

### Memorial Copy

Supervisor Tann did not save the Ilex corridor. That is why the stations still name bolts after her.

She kept the manifests honest after honesty stopped being profitable, and she made failure arrive in counted crates instead of panic. Her last authenticated signature authorized maintenance on a docking clamp for a ship that never came.

Continuity is not hope. It is what hope eats when the lights dim.
