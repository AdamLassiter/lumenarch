# Pio Marr

## Profile

Young deckhand, later a bridge figure between inherited ritual and living practice.

Pio started as a skeptical apprentice amused by elder doctrine. Gradually he becomes the sort of person who discovers, reluctantly, that the old warnings were written in blood.

He is curious, initially glib, eager to look unafraid, and learns seriousness the slow way. He wants to become genuinely competent without turning into the kind of elder he once mocked.

He writes private marginal notes after public lessons, jokes first, apologizes later, and repeats instructions under his breath when nervous.

## Backstory Fragment: Margin Notes from a Borrowed Manual

### Beside Section 4: Emergency Duct Closure

Elder Vale says never call a duct "just a pipe." A pipe carries something you asked for. A duct carries the difference between a room and a coffin.

I said this was dramatic. She made me inspect the west nursery bypass with the lights off.

Correction: she is dramatic and also correct.

### Beside Section 9: Public Apology Protocol

I told Fen the red lever was ceremonial.

It was not ceremonial.

No one died. Two people lost sleep. One pot of soup reached the ceiling. I am to clean vent grilles until my wrists understand civic trust.

### Loose Insert, Years Later

I caught myself saying "again" to a junior today.

She rolled her eyes exactly the way I used to, which was rude and historically justified. I nearly laughed. Then the pressure alarm chirped and her mouth moved through the phrase before her hands did.

Good. Let her hate me after the seal holds.
