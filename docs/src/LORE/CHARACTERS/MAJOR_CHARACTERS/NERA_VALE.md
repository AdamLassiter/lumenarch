# Nera Vale

## Profile

Quartermaster and safety elder of Spindle Mercy.

Nera was raised in a station culture where maintenance law, oral tradition, and grief discipline were inseparable. She inherited procedures from people who survived real decompression disasters and power failures, and she treats every "rite" as compressed survival technology.

She is dryly funny, severe in training contexts, deeply protective, and not interested in being admired. Her purpose is to keep useful truth alive in forms that people will actually remember under stress.

She teaches by making people repeat phrases aloud, taps plaques, seals, and valves while thinking, and uses embarrassment as a training tool, but never cruelty.

## Backstory Fragment: Training Recitation

### Spindle Mercy Safety Hall

"Say it again," Nera said.

Pio stared at the brass pressure plaque like it had insulted his family. "A hatch is a promise made twice. Once by the maker. Once by the hand that checks it."

"Again."

"Elder Vale, if I say it a fourth time, the hatch will begin to feel flattered."

"Good. Flattered hatches are easier to spot when they lie."

The apprentices laughed. Nera did not. She lifted a cracked seal from the tray and pressed it into Pio's palm until the crescent split marked his skin. Then she told them about Bay Four, about the song the survivors used to count children through smoke, about the engineer who forgot one phrase and opened the wrong side first.

No one laughed after that.

### Private Ledger Note

The words are surviving. I am less certain about the habits.

They can recite seal law, duct law, valve order, red-route etiquette. They bow at the plaques. They touch the clamps before crossing. Good children. Polite children.

But ritual can become a decorated empty tank. It looks full until someone needs air.

Tomorrow I will flood the practice lock with harmless fog and let them hate me.
