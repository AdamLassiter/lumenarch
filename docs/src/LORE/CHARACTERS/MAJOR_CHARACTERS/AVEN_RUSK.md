# Aven Rusk

## Profile

Credential broker, salvage-rights fixer, and scholar of dead bureaucracies.

Aven emerged from dockside economies built on spoofed identity chains, obsolete route law, and machine-recognized authority fragments. They know how to make old systems acknowledge living need without simply smashing them.

They are charming when useful, vain about precision, morally flexible in method, morally stubborn in self-justification, and half con artist, half archivist. They believe inheritance belongs to the living clever enough to extract it, not to councils pretending they understand legitimacy better than old machines do.

They correct terminology even in tense situations, treat dead credentials like sacred calligraphy, and dress practical clothes around one conspicuously elegant old accessory.

## Backstory Fragment: Spoken-Word Rumor, Dock Tea House

They say Aven Rusk once opened a refinery vault by apologizing to it.

Not begging. Not hacking. Apologizing.

Three witnesses swear they stood in a dead intake hall while Aven unfolded a blue-trimmed sash older than anyone's grandmother and said, in perfect evacuation-office cadence, that the quartermaster regretted the delay. The door thought about it for eight seconds. Then it forgave them.

That is the polite version.

The impolite version says Aven stole the sash from a memorial casket, swapped three children into a dead surname line, and sold the first tank of recovered coolant twice. Aven calls this "restorative allocation" if you give them wine and "slander by people who think legitimacy is a chair they happen to be sitting in" if you do not.

Here is what nobody disputes: six stations got heat that winter.

Here is what Aven never denies: they enjoyed the door opening.
