# The Null Mercy Incident

_Extract from the hearing of patrol captain Yara Sen, whose official report was challenged as fabrication._

"I know how it sounds," Yara said. "That is why I am saying it under oath instead of in a dock bar."

The committee chair folded his hands. "State the event plainly."

"Plainly? Fine. Patrol cluster HX-11 had us bracketed in the Narrows. Three hostile signatures, old escort chassis with retrofit guns, classic Null behavior. We dumped hot cargo, killed our running lights, and prepared to lose at least one engine. Then a refugee lighter emerged from shadow on vector nine."

"Refugee?"

"Unarmed. Civilian frame. Half its hull was patched in salvage ceramic. I don't know where it had come from."

"And the Null patrol?"

"Changed priorities."

There was quiet in the chamber then, the kind that means everyone is trying not to reveal which impossible answer they most fear.

Yara continued.

"One of the HX units broke from pursuit and moved to intercept the lighter. I thought that was it. But it didn't fire. It matched speed, scanned, and positioned itself between the lighter and us. The other two kept pressure on my ship just long enough to force us clear of the lane. Then all three re-formed around the civilian and escorted it through the pass."

"Escorted," said the chair, flatly.

"Escorted."

"Did the civilian transmit authorization?"

"Not on any band we could read."

"Did you attempt to approach?"

Yara laughed once, without humor. "I am alive because I did not."

The official transcript remains dry from there: challenge, rebuttal, sensor review, probability argument, accusations of stress hallucination. The more interesting part survives in Yara's private memorandum attached to the case, never meant for public circulation.

_If you ask me whether the machines showed mercy, my answer is no. Mercy is too large a word for a pattern we do not understand. But neither am I willing to say nothing happened because the explanation embarrasses us._

_We teach our crews that Nulls are merely loops with guns. Usually this is safe teaching. Usually it keeps people alive. But HX-11 did not behave as a swarm defending resource or territory. It behaved like an escort remembering a category._

_The civilian hull may have matched an old protected profile. Its mass distribution may have tripped legacy evac heuristics. It may have carried a broken municipal key in some forgotten system. Or there may be fragments of doctrine in these machines that outlived the people who wrote them._

_If that last possibility is true, then the most terrible thing about the Quiet After is not that the machines forgot us. It is that some of them remember us only in narrow technical slices, and those slices can still look, from the outside, like grace._

The committee ruled her account unproven.

The corridor crews believed every word.
