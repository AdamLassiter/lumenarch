# The Salvager Saint

_Presented as paired texts: a devotional reading used on Saint Braddan's Day, and a maintenance report attributed to the historical Braddan Vale._

## I. From the Devotional of Saint Braddan the Listener

Blessed Braddan, who heard the language of tired metal,

who laid his hand upon the hull and knew where it ached,

who divided spoil without envy and water without fear,

who taught that a ship is not owned by the loudest voice upon it,

but by the order in which its people save one another.

We remember the Miracle of the Four Seals, when the breach doors opened though the power was red.

We remember the Mercy of Dry Dock Seven, when he gave his own ration share for three weeks and no child on the ring starved.

We remember the Judgment of the Knife Crates, when armed claimants came to seize repair stock and departed with empty hands because Braddan spoke the truth so plainly that even desperate men could not bear to oppose it.

Blessed Braddan, teach our hands to listen and our greed to hesitate.

## II. Dockmaster's Incident Ledger, Dry Dock Seven

Day 19 of ration compression.

Children are not starving because I cut adult portions first and posted the numbers publicly so everyone had the same enemy.

The "Miracle of the Four Seals" occurred because Seal Two had never lost bus integrity; the indicator was dead. We bypassed the panel and drove the doors directly from maintenance crawl B. Toma nearly lost two fingers because he moved before I finished the count.

As for the knife-crate business:

There were nine men, not a mob. Four were armed, badly. They had already convinced themselves they were morally entitled to the laminates because they had lost more people on the route than we had. I told them entitlement does not patch hulls and that if they took the crates by force, every other ring would hear the story before they reached open dark. Then I had Rell open the outer shutters enough for them to see how thin our reserve actually was.

What persuaded them was arithmetic.

I am writing this because people have begun improving my decisions while I am still alive enough to be irritated by it.

No one listened to the hull. We inspected stress seams.

No one opened doors by faith. We found a live line.

No one shared rations because of virtue. We shared them because riots consume more calories than discipline.

Still, I suppose I understand the impulse. It is easier to love a saint than a quartermaster. Saints ask for reverence. Quartermasters ask for compliance, inventory, and smaller bowls.

If this ring survives me, I would prefer to be remembered accurately, but I have met history before and do not expect favors.

One thing may be said kindly:

fair division is not softness. It is a machine for surviving one another.

- Braddan Vale

## III. Marginal Note Added Much Later

The devotional is false in every detail except the important ones.
